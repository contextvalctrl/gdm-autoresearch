## Calibration Quality Analysis (Beyond Binary Score)

For candidates who pass, compute a simple Brier score from their stated probabilities:

**Brier score formula:**
`B = (1/N) × Σ (p_i − o_i)²`

Where:
- `p_i` = stated probability of Beat (expressed as a fraction, e.g., 0.75)
- `o_i` = 1 if Beat, 0 if Miss (actual outcome)
- N = 10 (number of questions)

**Interpretation:**
- Brier score 0.0 = perfect calibration
- Brier score 0.25 = random calibration (equivalent to always saying 50%)
- Brier score > 0.25 = worse than random (poorly calibrated)

**Target for Phase 1:** Prefer candidates with Brier score < 0.15 over candidates who scored 6/10 with extreme probabilities. A candidate who correctly identifies 7/10 at 60–70% confidence is more valuable than one who scores 7/10 but with 90%+ confidence on all correct answers (overfit to narrative, not genuine calibration signal).

## FAQ for Candidates (Include with Test)

**Q: What is EPS consensus?**
A: The average estimate from Wall Street analysts, as reported by financial data providers (Bloomberg, FactSet, etc.). The specific consensus used for grading is the value from Wall Street Horizon at market close 2 days before the earnings date.

**Q: What counts as a "beat"?**
A: Reported non-GAAP or adjusted EPS strictly greater than the consensus estimate. If the reported EPS exactly equals the consensus, it counts as a miss (≤ consensus = Miss).

**Q: Can I use any resources?**
A: Please do not look up the answers. The test measures your existing knowledge and calibration, not your ability to search. If you want to test your search-augmented accuracy, note which questions you looked up — we may use this as additional signal.

**Q: Why probability, not just binary?**
A: We're building a protocol that uses probability estimates, not binary calls. Your ability to express calibrated uncertainty (60% confident vs. 90% confident) is as important as your binary accuracy. The protocol rewards well-calibrated claims, not just correct directional calls.
