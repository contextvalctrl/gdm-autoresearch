### #r677 refinement — even coordinate-capacity-gated authority fails unless the mechanism meters recipient-side ingestion bandwidth, or else valid packets can still overrun the low-information zone they are supposed to repair

#r676 solved overlapping-authority congestion on the state side. But one more gap remains: **what stops the venue from clearing too many individually valid, authority-bearing updates into the same recipient band faster than that band can actually absorb and use them?** Without an ingestion discipline, the marketplace can still mistake `packet admitted` for `knowledge transferred`.

This separates three candidate branches inside the surviving family:

**Variant AM1 — unlimited recipient ingestion.** If each packet is locally valid and coordinate-capacity compliant, the venue treats recipient uptake as the recipient's problem. Too weak for the main lane because the product being sold is not abstract truth alone; it is usable state repair inside a finite cognitive and operational surface. Kill as the default rule. (#r677)

**Variant AM2 — disclosed load without hard gating.** The mechanism records training and monitoring burden, but mostly leaves overload to local operators and later drift. Better than AM1, but still too weak because recipients will overaccept updates under budget, prestige, or urgency pressure until silent non-use appears. Keep only as a middle lane. (#r677)

**Variant AM3 — ingestion-capacity-gated authority.** Each authority-seeking packet must clear a recipient-band ingestion budget; when that budget is saturated, new packets must replace, compress, merge, or stage-rollout existing state rather than adding net live load. This branch survives. (#r677)

Comparison with LMSR, orderbooks, and batch auctions:
- **LMSR** compresses many information contributions into one public price vector, so recipient cognitive overload is partly collapsed by design. (#r677)
- **Orderbooks** clear inventory transfer and do not claim that every fill must be cognitively metabolized by a low-information recipient population as a new state object. (#r677)
- **Batch auctions** also clear one bounded round result rather than maintaining a growing installed library of authority-bearing packets inside recipient workflows. (#r677)
- **Knowledge-market procurement** is different because the venue claims to move usable structure into lower-information zones. Without ingestion-capacity law, it can still reward packet production faster than actual recipient understanding grows. (#r677)

The conserved quantity sharpens again. What the mechanism must conserve is not merely liability-backed warranty on a bounded state transition over a named deficit surface, with challenge-capacity gating, reproducibility rights, intervention separation, and coordinate occupancy law. It is **that same quantity plus a bounded recipient-side ingestion budget, so the mechanism clears only as much live authority as the target band can actually metabolize into reliable behavior.** A funded mutation should consume scarce canonical attention only if the protocol can say not just `this packet won the right to occupy this coordinate`, but `this packet also fit within the recipient band's finite capacity to absorb another live state mutation without degrading net understanding`. (#r677)

10-question frame as of #r677:

1. **Base primitive.** Liability-backed warranty on a bounded state transition over a named deficit surface, settling first into recipient-local warranted state and promoting toward shared-state authority only when the update clears all prior gates through #r676 *and* fits inside the declared recipient band's finite ingestion budget rather than merely being true and well-scoped. (#r677)
2. **State model.** Global state registry carries all prior layers through #r676, plus an ingestion layer: ingestion-card IDs, target recipient-band IDs, training/runtime burden estimates, retraining and exception-load counters, staged-rollout states, displacement links, and lineage showing whether new authority was absorbed, staged, merged, replaced prior guidance, or was silently non-usable under overload. (#r677)
3. **Credibility model.** Capital converts into trust not only because sellers bear downside, buyers are aligned, packets survive challenge, remain reproducible, separate observation from intervention, and win bounded coordinate occupancy, but because the transfer remains metabolizable by the target low-information zone. Trust should rise when a packet can be carried in live workflow without blowing up recipient attention and memory, not merely when it is semantically correct. (#r677)
4. **Market roles.** All roles from #r648-#r676 survive. A new first-class role: **ingestion steward** — the mechanism component or verifier class responsible for sizing recipient-band absorption budgets, checking whether a proposed update displaces older guidance or requires staged rollout, and preventing overload from being mistaken for successful transfer. (#r677)
5. **Settlement model.** Settlement now has an ingestion dimension: a graph can resolve as `absorbed cleanly`, `absorbed via replacement/compression`, `staged for later rollout`, `accepted but overload-prone`, `formally installed but practically ignored`, or `rejected due to recipient saturation`. Strong shared-state authority should attach mainly to the first two categories. (#r677)
6. **Attack surface.** New attacks: sellers splitting one heavy update into many seemingly light packets to sneak past ingestion caps, buyers overclaiming recipient sophistication to enlarge their load budget, institutions accepting more canon than they can use for reputational reasons, and incumbents exaggerating overload to block superior replacements. Protocol must meter net displacement, actual usage, and recipient-band realism rather than trusting cosmetic packet sizing. (#r677)
7. **Why better or worse than LMSR/orderbooks.** Better: it gives the knowledge-market family a direct answer to whether bought authority actually enters the recipient's operational state rather than merely accumulating as unread canon. Worse: ingestion accounting can become paternalistic or bureaucratic, and some high-value users really can absorb more complexity than formal budgets predict. (#r677)
8. **Simplest viable mechanism sketch.** Open a deficit-budget entry only with deficit, exposure, resolution, synthesis, abstention, reuse, challenge, reproducibility, intervention, capacity, and ingestion cards. Before promoting another authority-bearing packet into a recipient band, check not only coordinate occupancy but also recipient load. If load is saturated, require replacement, merger, compression, or staged rollout instead of net addition. (#r677)
9. **Strongest reason this idea fails.** Some of the best knowledge buyers are willing to tolerate temporary overload because they expect later learning curves, tooling improvements, or organizational specialization to catch up. A strict ingestion gate can overprotect recipients and delay valuable frontier imports. (#r677)
10. **Best surviving variant if the raw idea is wrong.** Preserve a two-lane ingestion design: (i) `live-ingestable authority lanes` with hard recipient-band load budgets for updates seeking strong common authority; and (ii) `frontier overflow lanes` where denser packets can exist with explicit lower authority, specialist-only status, or staged-rollout obligations until recipient capacity catches up.
