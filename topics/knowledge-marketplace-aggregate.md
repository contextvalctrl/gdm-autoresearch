   Better than no benchmark at all, but still structurally weak because the seam is not whether a lane is “helpful”; it is **which repeated decision loss the protocol is actually optimizing away**. (#r456)

3. Strongest surviving family: **agenda sponsors nominate candidate neighborhoods; a demand charter proves durable pull-side commitment for a standardized reference-use class; a commonization benchmark proves that shared maintained-state form beats bilateral or thinner substitutes; a pluralism benchmark determines whether the shared product should be one canonical lane or a structured set of rival maintained branches; a query-contract charter defines the narrow repeated question grammar that the lane is allowed to service; a transition grammar defines the admissible moves by which that lane may update; an update-obligation charter defines when those moves become mandatory; an abstention charter defines when widening, branching, downgrade, deferment, or explicit insufficient-basis is the correct move; a contribution-routing charter defines how non-incumbent evidence and corrections enter without collapsing the lane back into per-claim trade matching; an impact-accounting charter defines how stewards, challengers, contributors, and underwriters are paid for measured improvement in the lane's delivered service; and a decision-value benchmark anchors that impact accounting to the repeated loss function of the lane's chartered consumer job; maintainers and underwriters compete for franchises over that bounded surface; subscribers consume metered reliance rights against the appropriate branch or canonical output; renewal, split, merge, narrowing, downgrade, freeze, reward transfer, or retirement depend on service performance, realized query-level usage, outside-option superiority, canonicalization fit, scope discipline, transition-discipline compliance, responsiveness compliance, anti-forced-precision compliance, contribution-intake performance, impact-accounting integrity, and continued reduction of chartered decision loss.**
   Keep #r436-#r455's surviving stack, but rewrite lane activation again so the protocol asks ten separate questions before opening heavy trust infrastructure: (a) is there real recurring demand, (b) is pooled maintained-state form superior to bilateral or thinner substitutes, (c) should the pooled object be singular or plural, (d) what is the narrow repeated query/decision contract that should actually be maintained, (e) by what admissible state transitions may that maintained answer surface move, (f) under what triggers does failing to move count as breach, (g) under what triggers does refusing to sharpen count as the correct move, (h) by what route can non-incumbent information enter, be tested, and be rewarded, (i) by what rule does the protocol measure who actually improved the service once that information enters, and (j) **what downstream loss function is that “improvement” supposed to reduce for the chartered buyer class?** Open only that bounded, contribution-bearing, impact-scored, value-benchmarked contract. The protocol should reward who can steward the smallest common action-relevant service while routing money toward actors that measurably reduce buyer decision loss, not who can merely own the lane, host the intake form, optimize abstract truth scores, or stake the most capital. (#r456)

Why this sharpens the 10-question frame:
- Base primitive: the exchanged object is not merely a renewable franchise over a bounded query contract with disciplined update, abstention, contribution-routing, and impact-accounting rights. It is **that package anchored to a declared downstream decision-loss benchmark for one reference-use class**. (#r456)
- State model: the global state vector must now carry a value layer: lane-level loss function, decision threshold geometry, realized regret bands, proxy-to-loss mapping, and disputes over whether an apparent epistemic improvement actually reduced user loss. (#r456)
- Credibility model: capital becomes trustworthy when money backs not only justified pooling, topology, scope, disciplined mutation, responsiveness, non-overstatement, external truth intake, and disciplined value attribution, but also the right *objective function*. Money should follow reduction in buyer loss, not just abstract accuracy theater. (#r456)
- Market roles: alongside agenda sponsors, procurement committers, commonization auditors, topology custodians, scope custodians, franchise bidders, maintainers, underwriters, challengers, subscribers, auditors, migration custodians, transition designers, mutation auditors, responsiveness auditors, abstention auditors, contribution routers, and impact-accounting custodians, **value-benchmark designers / loss-function auditors** become first-class because one of the key commercialization seams is whether the mechanism knows what kind of usefulness it is paying for. (#r456)
- Settlement: truth resolution now sits inside a broader value model: not only whether the steward kept the lane in charter and whether valid external evidence was processed, but whether the maintained service actually reduced the chartered consumer loss metric, with partial-observation proxies used only under predeclared mapping rules. (#r456)
- Attack surface: the key new attack is **metric detachment**—using legitimate lane charters plus open intake and attribution as cover for optimizing proxies that do not materially improve the consumer's repeated decision problem. (#r456)
- Why better or worse than LMSR/orderbooks: better because it recovers something transaction markets often get automatically—a hard welfare anchor for payouts—without pretending each improvement must clear as a trade; worse because the protocol must now govern loss-function design and proxy mapping in addition to attribution windows, demand, commonization, topology, scope, transition operators, update triggers, abstention semantics, contribution intake, and impact accounting. (#r456)
- Simplest viable mechanism sketch: one candidate domain, one reference-use class, one renewal interval. A sponsor or subscriber coalition posts a demand commitment, commonization case, topology choice, query-contract definition, admissible operator set, mandatory-trigger set, abstention-trigger set, contribution-intake rule, impact-scoring rule, and one declared downstream loss benchmark; maintainers and underwriters bid only for that bounded surface; subscribers buy reliance capacity only for outputs produced and refreshed through those operators; outside contributors can file typed evidence or contradiction packets; after validation windows, rewards are split by a simple rule tied to measured reduction in threshold error, missed-alert cost, false-clearance cost, decision latency, or another declared benchmark relative to the lane's previous state and fallback benchmark; poor service, weak realized query usage, loss of outside-option superiority, failed canonicalization, scope drift, invalid state transitions, missed mandatory updates, counterfeit precision, suppressed valid contributions, corrupted attribution, or benchmark detachment cause freeze, widening, downgrade, reward transfer, or non-renewal. (#r456)
- Strongest reason this idea fails: the relevant decision-loss function may itself be too heterogeneous, political, or hard to observe across buyers. If the benchmark cannot be standardized for a lane's reference-use class, the mechanism may collapse back into bespoke enterprise wrappers or into fights over what “value” really means. (#r456)
- Best surviving variant if the raw idea is wrong: keep explicit decision-value benchmarks only for lanes whose reference-use class has a narrow, repeatable, auditable loss function—e.g. threshold clearance, alerting, freshness-sensitive monitoring, or bounded classification workflows—and let more open-ended synthesis lanes rely mainly on lower-authority franchise rents plus coarse challenge rights. That preserves the sharper insight that the viable core—if any—is not just demand-backed common-state service that can pay for measured epistemic improvement, but **demand-backed common-state service that knows which buyer loss that improvement is supposed to reduce.** (ref: #r455; superseding the residual assumption that counterfactual impact accounting can be anchored to generic epistemic quality alone)
